Reflection

It has been almost four years since I first started my adventure here at Iowa State University. I have learned so much during my current time here. I remember taking countless visits during high school to Iowa State. Not because I couldn’t make up my mind, but because I couldn’t wait to get to come here. I enjoyed the campus, the students, the professors, and the atmosphere of Ames as a whole.

My first semester should be entitled “Responsibilities”. Not because I was responsible but because the lack of responsibility I had and the time it spent for me to gain that characteristic. Friends and myself would joke that the true definition of an engineer is just someone who knows how to properly “Google” the solution. That attitude was one that I grew out of eventually with the help of family, friends, and Iowa State. Group activities helped me grow to be responsible as well as form leadership skills. Countless semester long projects, Senior Design, and club activities and hackathons allowed me to form my own individual opinions while still contributing and learning what it is like to work in a professional environment (as well as my countless internships).

Like I had mentioned earlier, engineering is not simply “Googling” for an answer. An engineer has to usually solve problems that have never been solved before. When I get to my career path, I know that I will have the resources I need to solve these types of problems, because Iowa State, its faculty, and my peers have all given me the resources and understanding to not only solve these problems on my own, but learn when I need help. Classes like 309, 329, and 339 have all helped me further understand the workflow of a software engineer. Project plans, design processes, methodologies, and architecture have all been taught in a way that I will be able to strategically use them in my career continuously and already have started to.

Iowa State hasn’t only helped me academically. In the past four years I have gotten to know so many new people. From professors, students, peers, friends, roommates, and mentors, all have contributed vastly to my experience at Iowa State. Being able to freely go to a professor’s office for help, whether related to class work or personal work, is a resource that I will surely miss.

One of my favorite experiences was during my senior year. Being able to travel on a charter bus to Michigan, stay up for three days straight, program with some of the smartest and brightest people currently in college, while building friendships was one I will surely remember. That event was MHACKS. Many other insightful experiences happened at Iowa State such as VEISHA and the Career Fairs, but if everyone just does the average actives during their experience, then where is the adventure?

If I were to live my four years over again, I would hardly change anything. I would want to know my freshman year that academics are important so as not to neglect them, but realize there needs to be a balance of learning and fun. My first year I was eager to go home when I could, when I could have spent more time making new friends and learning more. One more thing that professor David Weiss had mentioned was to seek out a mentor. He had an academic mentor that he could go to and met with once a month during his undergraduate and recommended the same. Although I had spiritual mentors, I never had thought about one on an academic level.

Overall Iowa State has been quite the adventure. I chose mine well. I found my passion in a career, formed new interests, met lifelong friends, and even married the love of my life. Iowa State has gone far beyond providing me the resources to achieve my academic goals. This university has been given me the materials to advance on my life long goals.

Objective

Goals

My ideal career would be to able to build something that will not only get used daily and help people, but something new and inventive. That is one of the reasons why I chose Software Engineering in the first place. Joining a startup company is not only fun but challenging. During my time at Iowa State I have been given the tools to do that through numerous courses with economics, public speaking, and culture.

General Education

Many courses have been valuable to my learning experience outside of my major as well. Most students will initially think of it as a waste of time to take such classes. However when you are 18 years old you have no idea what kind of career and life you have planned. Classes such as Speech, Economics, and World Religions all helped me today. They have allowed me to better myself to be personable and not only tech minded.

Beyond Engineering

One thing I was fortunate enough to have were classes that expanded on current events and using those to teach lessons. When you are constantly surrounded by engineering peers and advisors, its hard to see the overall life perspective. Being taught about ethics during a time of NSA spying, professionalism when the national health care site launched and I'm sure many other lessons, has helped shaped my context of the industry in a more broad sense.
